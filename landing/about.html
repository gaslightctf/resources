About us

We started playing CTFs in 2024, beginning, like many secondary school students, with picoCTF, a beginner-friendly cybersecurity competition hosted by Carnegie Mellon University. With its approachable challenges and extensive learning resources, picoCTF introduced us to the world of cybersecurity and competitive hacking. Since then, we’ve participated in dozens of CTFs, many of which can be found on our team’s CTFTime profile.

As we became more involved in the CTF community, we noticed that many student-focused competitions and their prize pools were primarily aimed at participants in the United States. This imbalance is also reflected on CTFTime: as of May 11, 2026, there are only 6 ranked teams from the UK compared to 76 from the US.

That’s why we decided to create our own competition: a beginner-friendly CTF designed for secondary school students primarily in the UK, but also around the world. In an increasingly digital world, we believe cybersecurity is not only an essential skill, but also an exciting and rewarding field that we hope more students should have the opportunity to explore, similar to us.
